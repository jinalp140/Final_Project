UPMA

Alternative names Uppuma, Uppittu, Uppumavu, Uppindi, Kharabath, Upeet, Rulanv Course Breakfast Place of origin India Region or state South India, Western India Upma, uppumavu or uppittu is a dish from the Indian subcontinent, most common in South Indian,[1] Maharashtrian, and Sri Lankan Tamil breakfast; cooked as a thick porridge from dry roasted semolina or coarse rice flour.[2] Various seasonings and/or vegetables are often added during the cooking, depending on individual preferences. Today it is popular in most parts of India and Canada is prepared in various ways.
